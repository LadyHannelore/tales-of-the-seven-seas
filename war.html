War Declaration

Ready to fight some wars? You may declare war against one nation to capture land, cities, loot, or gain diplomatic favors. To declare, select one of four casus belli. This determines victory requirements and outcomes. After choosing, create a war ticket, mention your opponent, and attach a map of your target tiles.

Important: Both sides enter a mutual two-week cooldown after war ends. You cannot declare a new war while already at war or during cooldown.

Casus Belli

  • Skirmish: Capture 1–20 tiles. Victory: hold all by turn 4 or opponent surrenders. Defeat: opponent demands 1–20 tiles they hold at turn 4.
  • Conquest: Capture 1–3 settlements (excluding capitals). Victory: seize 1–5 settlements by turn 6. +3 tiles per settlement. Defeat: lose same.
  • Raid: Capture 1–15 loot items. Victory: seize 1–15 loot by turn 4. Defeat: opponent seizes 1–15 loot.
  • Subjugation: Subjugate opponent under conditions. Victory: capture capital by turn 6. Defeat: opponent demands reparations or subjugation.

Armies & Units

Create an army by spending 1 labor (this halts labor regen). At war start, distribute armies by settlement population (1 for town, 3 for city, 5 for metropolis). Each army (max 10 units) begins with:

  • 5 Infantry units
  • 1 Commander unit

Purchase up to two unit upgrades per army:

  • 1 Bronze → 3 Shock units
  • 1 Timber → 3 Archer units
  • 1 Mount → 4 Cavalry units
  • 1 Mount → 2 Chariot units

Maneuvering & Battle

Wars proceed in 1–3 day turns:

  1. Maneuver: Move armies up to 3 tiles (land) or as allowed by ship tech. Exiting expansion range costs food or mounts. Allied territory requires permission to avoid extra cost.
  2. Battle: Armies on the same tile may initiate war chess on a 9×9 board. Each unit has Attack, Defense, and Movement stats; battles resolve via placement, combat, and rally phases.
  3. No Action: If either side is inactive for 3 consecutive turns (skips actions or no response within 3 days), the war ends.
  4. Turn Limit: A war automatically ends after 10 turns regardless of activity.

Sieges & Treaties

Sieges occur on settlement tiles; unguarded cities fall automatically. Siege battles use the same war chess rules on a 4×9 board. Loot is granted per settlement tier.

Treaties allow diplomatic deals. Post in worldbuilding chat and sign. They have no automatic effect unless enforced by writing tech or as peace treaties.
