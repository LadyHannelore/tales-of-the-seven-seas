War System

Master the art of conflict and conquest in Tales of the Seven Seas

Casus Belli (War Types)

To declare war, you must select one of four casus belli, which determines your victory requirements and conditions. Create a war ticket, mention your opponent, and declare your intentions.

Skirmish

Declaration: Declare 1-20 tiles to capture

Victory Requirement: Hold >50% of declared tiles by end of Turn 4 OR opponent surrenders

Victory: Capture declared tiles + 1 loot per 3 tiles held

Defeat: Opponent may demand 50% of undeclared tiles they hold

Conquest

Declaration: Declare 1-3 settlements to capture (no capitals unless only city)

Victory Requirement: Capture settlements within 6 turns

Victory: Gain settlements + 3 tiles/settlement (must maintain territorial contiguity)

Defeat: Lose settlements + opponent may relocate orphaned tiles

Raid

Declaration: Declare loot target (1-15)

Victory Requirement: Generate loot through battles/sieges within 4 turns

Victory: Gain declared loot

Defeat: Pay opponent 1/2 declared loot value

Subjugation

Declaration: State conditions (vassalization, cultural change, etc.)

Victory Requirement: Capture capital OR hold 50% settlements for 2 turns

Victory: Enforce conditions + 1 settlement/3 tiles/2 loot

Defeat: Opponent may demand 1 settlement OR 3 tiles OR 2 loot

Army System

Army Creation & Costs

Creation Cost: 1 Labor (halts regeneration only during active battle turns)

Maintenance: After Turn 3, costs 1 Food/army/turn

Settlement Limits

  • Town: 1 army
  • City: 3 armies
  • Metropolis: 5 armies

Maneuvering

Movement Rules

Movement: 3 tiles/turn (land/water)

Foreign Territory: Always costs 1 Food or 1 Mount per army (even if approved)

Stacking Limit: Max 3 armies/tile (-1 move/combat per excess army)

Inactivity Penalties

  • 1 missed turn: Opponent gains +1 movement
  • 2 missed turns: Auto-loss of skirmishes/raids

Battle System: War Chess

Deployment

  • Max 12 units/battle (reinforcements arrive Turn 3)
  • Grid: 9×9 (attacker: rows 1-2, defender: rows 8-9)

Combat Rules

  • Archers: Must be stationary to fire (2-tile range)
  • Cavalry: Flanking bonus vs Shock units
  • Commanders: Death causes -1 movement to all units
  • Chariots: Require rider unit (infantry default)

Battle Phases

  1. Placement (Red → Blue)
  2. Combat (Alternating turns): Move/Turn/Attack (Archers only)
  3. Rally: Merge surviving units into new army

Sieges

Capture Requirements

  • Army must occupy settlement for 1 full turn
  • Must eliminate all defenders OR kill commander

Loot Values

Settlement Loot Equivalent Value
Town 1 15 coins OR 1 labor OR 1 common resource*
City 4 60 coins OR 4 labor OR 4 common resources
Metropolis 9 135 coins OR 9 labor OR 9 common resources

*Common resources: Grain, Timber, Stone (no strategic resources)

New War Mechanics

Supply Lines

Armies beyond expansion range 2+ turns: Lose 1 random unit/turn

War Weariness

After Turn 4: +1 Food maintenance/army/turn

After Turn 6: -1 Combat Strength to all units

Diplomatic Penalties

Aggressor loses 1 Diplomatic Favor per war declaration

Scouting

1 free Scout unit per army:

  • Movement: 5 tiles
  • Reveals enemy positions in adjacent tiles
  • Cannot attack/be attacked

War Restrictions (Updated)

  • Max 1 active war/nation
  • 14-day cooldown after war with same opponent
  • New players: 14-day protection after spawn

Players may refuse:

  • Wars during existing conflicts
  • Full annexation demands
  • Subjugation violating core RP values

Surrender Terms:

  • Maximum demands capped at 200% war cost
  • Moderator approval required for asymmetric terms

Example War Flow

Conquest Example

  1. Declare Conquest (3 cities) → Pay 1 Labor to raise army
  2. Maneuver cavalry (3 tiles/turn) → Pay 1 Mount for foreign movement
  3. Siege city (12-unit limit battle):
    • Turn 1: Archers fire from stationary positions
    • Turn 2: Cavalry flank shock units
    • Turn 3: Reinforcements arrive
  4. After capture:
    • Gain city + 3 tiles (ensuring contiguity)
    • Claim 4 loot → Convert to 60 coins
  5. Post-war: 14-day cooldown, pay 1 Diplomatic Favor
