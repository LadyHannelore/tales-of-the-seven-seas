War System

Ready to fight some wars? You may declare war against 1 nation to capture land, cities, loot (check siege section), or gain diplomatic favors.

War Declaration

To declare a war you must select 1 of 4 casus belli, which will determine your victory requirement, and your victory and defeat conditions. When a victory requirement is met, a war ends with the victor gaining the victory condition. If the victory requirement is not met, the opponent will gain the defeat condition.

After selecting a casus belli, create a war ticket, mention your opponent, and create your war declaration message.

Casus Belli List

Skirmish

Declaration: Declare 1-20 tiles on the map to capture

Victory requirement: Hold all these tiles by the end of the 4th turn, or opponent surrenders

Victory condition: Capture 1-20 tiles per declaration

Defeat condition: Opponent may demand 1-20 tiles on the map which they hold by the end of the 4th turn

Conquest

Declaration: Declare 1-3 settlements on the map to capture (excluding capital, unless it's their only city)

Victory requirement: Capture 1-5 settlements within 6 turns, or opponent surrenders

Victory condition: Gain 1-5 settlements per declaration + 3 tiles per settlement (must not left opponent with tiles outside their expansion range, unless necessary)

Defeat condition: Lost 1-5 settlements which the opponent manage to hold by the end of the 6th turn + 3 tiles per settlement

Raid

Declaration: Capture 1-15 loot

Victory requirement: Capture 1-15 loot within 4 turns, or opponent surrenders

Victory condition: 1-15 loot

Defeat condition: Opponent gain 1-15 loot they manage to capture by the end of the 4th turn

Subjugation

Declaration: Subjugate the opponent to follow certain conditions (vassalization, culture conversion, stop doing roleplay offensive to your nation, others as approved by mod)

Victory requirement: Capture opponent's capital within 6 turns

Victory condition: Subjugate the opponent to follow the declaration condition + 1 settlement/1-6 tiles/1-3 loot

Defeat condition: Opponent may demand 1 settlement (within expansion range), 1-6 tiles, 1-3 loot, or other favors as reparations (approved by mod), or subjugate the opponent if their capital is captured within 6 turns

War Restrictions

  • You may only declare 1 war at a time
  • After you finish a war, you will not be able to declare on the same opponent for 2 weeks, but the opponent can declare on you
  • Players will be automatically protected 2 weeks after spawning
  • Players may refuse a full annexation, or a war declaration if they are already in a war

Army System

Armies will let you conquer cities, raid, and protect your land. You may create an army with 1 labor, which will stop the labor from regenerating until the war ends.

At the beginning of a war, both sides may distribute their armies around their settlements however they like, at a maximum equal to that settlement's population (1 for town, 3 for cities, 5 for metropolis).

Army Composition

Each army consist of individual units you may use in battle or maneuver across the battlefield. 1 army automatically start with 5 infantry units and 1 commander unit. The player may purchase 2 more modifications per army at the following cost, adding those units to your army. You may purchase these modifications at any time during the war.

1 bronze: 3 shock unit
1 timber: 3 archer unit
1 mount: 4 cavalry unit
1 mount: 2 chariot unit

Maneuvering

Each war is divided into turns, one turn lasting 1-3 days. For each turn, you may choose to:

  • Maneuver: Move each army 3 tiles by land (or water after unlocking shipbuilding). If the army move outside of their nation's expansion range (3 tiles around each settlement), they must pay 1 food per 3 tile movement, or 1 mount for 6 tile movement. 2 armies may occupy the same tile
  • Battle
  • Do nothing

Maneuver Rules

  • If both players choose to do nothing for 2 turns, the war will end
  • If a player does not respond in their turn after 3 days, they are considered as doing nothing unless specified later
  • If an army maneuvers through foreign territory, that player may choose to approve of the maneuver, start a war with the maneuvering army, or fund the maneuvering army by trading them goods
  • They may not join the war themselves against the same target
  • If the nation approves, your maneuvering will not count as being beyond expansion range and thus does not need extra food

Battle System

When 2 or more armies occupy the same tile, any of them may decide to start a battle. This is done using a board game, war chess, where each unit will move on a 9×9 board to eliminate all enemy units on the board.

Unit Types

Each unit has the following property in war chess: Attack, Defense, and movement.

Infantry

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front

Defense: None

Movement: 1 tile in any directions

Shock

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front

Defense: Immune to attacks single from infantry, cavalry, and commander, may be killed by multiple attacks from infantry, cavalry, and commander. Can be injured but cannot die from injury

Movement: 1 tile in any directions

Archer

Attack: May select "fire arrow" action, and select any target within a 3 tile firing range. When firing at a target, they will be injured. An injured target will have a slightly weaker defense than their regular counterparts

Defense: None

Movement: 1 tile in 4 cardinal directions

Commander

Must have 1 per color

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front

Defense: Immune to a single attacks by infantry and cavalry. May die from multiple attacks by infantry and cavalry, or from 1 shock. Immune to injury

Movement: 1 tile in any directions

Cavalry

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front

Defense: None

Movement: 3 tiles in any directions

Light Chariot

Chariot with infantry or archer rider

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front + fire arrow for archer rider

Defense: Immune to single attacks from infantry, cavalry, commander. May die from multiple attacks. Immune to shock attack in front. May die from shock attack from the side or back

Movement: 3 tiles in 4 directions

Heavy Chariot

Chariot with shock or commander rider

Attack: Automatically attack 1 tile in front

Defense: Immune to 1-2 attacks from infantry, cavalry, commander. May die from 3 or more attacks. Immune to 1 shock attack. May die from 2 or more shock attacks

Battle Phases

War chess is divided into 3 phases, placement, battle, and rally phase. The aggressor will be red, and the defender will be blue.

Placement Phase

Starting with red, the player may place their unit anywhere on the 5×2 area on their side of the board. Then the blue player will get their turn.

If you have more than 1 army within the tile, you may place all army's unit on the battlefield, but with only 1 commander. When placing a chariot unit, each chariot must have 1 rider unit being either infantry, archer, shock, or commander. Infantry unit serve as a default rider for the chariot and will not subtract from your infantry unit.

Battle Phase

Red player gets their turn first. Each turn, you may do 1 of 4 action per unit:

  • Move the unit
  • Turn the unit to face any 4 directions
  • Attack: for archers only
  • Nothing

After you have moved all units, you may end your turn. Your movement cannot be canceled. Then, the death of each side's unit will be calculated.

Rally Phase

At the end of a battle, each side will calculate the remaining units in their army:

Remaining units = Surviving units + Retreated units (your own) + captured units (from the opponent)

If your remaining units no longer have a commander, it will be added to 1 army of your choice. Your remaining units will constitute your army, which you may continue to use to fight the war.

Siege

Sieges constitute a special kind of battle, centered around settlements. When opposing armies are on a settlement tile, they may declare a siege. The siege will be fought with all armies in the settlement tile, including those who never left the settlement. If the settlement is unguarded, it is automatically captured.

In a siege, your army may be placed anywhere on the 4×9 area on your side of the board.

If a commander is no longer from the board, the opponent automatically conquers the settlement. If they do, they will gain 1 loot per town, 4 per city, and 9 per metropolis. Each loot represents goods you can demand from your opponent.

Loot Values

1 loot can be demanded for:

  • 1 labor
  • 30 coins
  • 2 resources of your choice

The goods you selected must be owned by the opposing nation.
