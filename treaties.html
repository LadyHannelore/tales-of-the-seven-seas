Treaties & Diplomacy

Treaty System

Not every problem needs a violent solution. Treaties allow you to make diplomatic deals with any nation you meet in your region or through exploration, ranging from roleplay deals to exchanging goods or ending conflicts. Unlike regular dealings, treaties will let you put your deals in stone.

Important: Treaties will NOT do the actions themselves, such as trading land, and you must follow their effect separately.

Creating Treaties

Treaty Process

  1. Treaties must be posted in worldbuilding chat, and could be graded as roleplay if the worldbuilding mod deems it appropriate
  2. All parties must sign the treaty
  3. You may invite third parties to sign the treaty as "witnesses"

Treaty Enforcement

Treaties have no mechanical effect (other than being graded as roleplay), and the effect must be enforced by its signers. This may range from:

  • Punishment from the witness, such as an invasion or losing status from unofficial alliances
  • Trade embargos or technological uncooperation
  • Any action you can do yourself is allowed

Mechanical Enforcement

Writing Technology Requirement

If at least one of the players signing the treaty unlock writing technology, the treaty may gain mechanical enforcement. This is when the treaty is officially enforced by the mods and cannot be broken. No unrelated players may sign a mechanically enforced treaty.

Peace Treaties

Peace treaties, treaties used to end wars, are automatically mechanically enforced whether the player has unlocked writing or not. You must post the treaty in your war ticket. The treaty's effect will immediately be enforced after both parties sign.

Diplomatic Examples

Trade Agreements

Establish long-term trade relationships, exclusive resource deals, or trade route protections between nations.

Non-Aggression Pacts

Agree to refrain from declaring war on each other for a specified period, promoting regional stability.

Alliance Treaties

Form military or economic alliances, agreeing to mutual defense or shared technological advancement.

Cultural Exchanges

Share cultural practices, technologies, or establish diplomatic marriages between ruling families.

Territorial Agreements

Define borders, establish colonial rights, or agree on expansion zones to prevent conflicts.

War Reparations

End conflicts with compensation packages, territory transfers, or ongoing tribute payments.

Best Practices

Writing Effective Treaties

  • Be Specific: Clearly define all terms, conditions, and expectations
  • Set Duration: Specify how long the treaty will remain in effect
  • Include Consequences: Define what happens if the treaty is broken
  • Involve Witnesses: Third-party nations can help ensure compliance
  • Plan Enforcement: Consider how you'll enforce the treaty without mechanical support

Diplomatic Considerations

  • Treaties can be valuable roleplay opportunities worth significant coin rewards
  • Consider your nation's culture and values when negotiating
  • Remember that reputation matters in long-term diplomatic relationships
  • Use treaties to create interesting narrative opportunities
