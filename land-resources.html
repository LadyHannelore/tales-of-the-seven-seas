Land & Resources

Mod: arthur

Territory

Each player will have a territory on the map, marked by their color on a tile. You may only construct settlements, worldbuild, or spawn resources within your territory.

Expansions

Expansions are how you grow your territory peacefully — no wars needed. Cost 20 coins per tile (lower for herder lifestyle). The land you will expand into must be:

  • Unoccupied (unless farmer expanding into gatherer or herder land)
  • Within settlement range (3 tiles by land, 3 tiles by calm sea with shipbuilding unlocked, 5 tiles by sea with open sea sailing unlocked)
  • Visible on your region's map, including up to 3 tiles outside the regional border. Must provide a map of your expansion by marking your region's map
  • Not of impassable terrain (ice, high mountains, etc)
  • Contiguous with existing land on the map if expanding by land
  • Sea expansion must be done from a coastal occupied tile to a coastal unoccupied tile within your sea settlement range

Expansion Window

From after the map is posted to Friday of the following week. Expansions posted outside the window will be deleted.

Resources

There are 4 resource types you may spawn from your land:

Food

Automatically spawn from tiles you have domestic lifeforms in (gatherers generate food from every tile). Will regenerate each cycle (14 days), depleting the food leftover from the last cycle.

Formula: food per tile = lifestyle modifier × terrain modifier

Total food: [farmland + (domestic lifeforms × 2) + native terrain food(gatherer) - population] rounded

Food is required for feeding your population, exploration and construction.

Spawned Resources

Can be spawned from your land using 1 labor and 1 tile as designated. Each spawnable tile may only be used once for spawning per cycle. Spawnable resources will not deplete at the end of the cycle. Must submit each spawn in the resource chat.

  • Timber: spawnable from all forest terrain tiles. Used in construction
  • Copper: spawnable from copper tiles. Used in construction and making bronze
  • Tin: spawnable from tin tiles. Used in making bronze
  • Mount: spawnable from any tile your mount is native to. Used in exploration and warfare
  • Books: spawnable from metropolis. Used in trading technologies

Bronze

1 copper and 1 tin can be converted into 2 bronze. Used in construction and warfare. Must submit each spawn in the resource chat. Will not deplete at the end of each cycle.

Unique Resource

Unique resources are counted by type instead of amount, denoted by the resource name in the player sheet. Each cycle, each player may post a minimum 200 word roleplay to create a unique resource to their nation, ranging from rare gems and local textile to even cultural services or tourism that may grow your economy.

Once approved, you may trade this resource to any player infinitely, while keeping your own, but this will count as 1 type (1 name). Used in construction, numbers indicate types. Will not deplete at the end of each cycle.

Trade System

Want to trade your goods with others? You can! You may trade with any player whose settlement is within your trade range.

Tradeable Goods

  • Coins
  • Any resources
  • Labor
  • Any personal creation or services

Trade Routes

By Land

Trade path must be between 2 settlements by land. Can cross over rivers, but not lakes or sea (water bodies that stretch across multiple tiles).

By Sea

Trade path must be between 2 settlements by sea. You must unlock shipbuilding technology to trade by calm sea, and must unlock open sea sailing to trade by open sea.

By River

Trade path must be between 2 settlements by the same river (connected without having to cross land or the ocean). Can be of any length, including outside the trade range.

Trade Types

Direct trade: Between you and the target nation through 1 route. Target nation must be within your trade range (or on the same river).

Indirect trade: Passes through another nation's territory (the middleman). Each pair of settlements must be within trade range of each other. The middleman may block the trade, tax the trade, or take the goods as they want. If a middleman does not respond within 24 hours, the trade automatically goes through.

Trade Format

@ Player 1, resource gain, resource lost 
@ Player 2, resource gain, resource lost 
@ Middleman
